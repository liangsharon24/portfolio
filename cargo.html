Cargo

A solution that provides more efficiency and better experience of grocery shopping.

Project Overview

An individual project aims to make grocery shopping more convenient for students. This project contains a thorough process of interaction design, from need finding to prototype designing.

Design Process

Step 1. Need Finding

Interview

From 6 interviews, I identified grocery shopping as one of the primary challenges for students who did not have a car and lived far away from a grocery store.

Step 2. Analysis

Persona

Based on the interviews, I created personas to help me think through the motivations, needs, and frustrations that might be experienced by potential users.

Comparative Analysis

I compared 3 kinds of services to identify the possible solution to my problem statement, trying to discover the niche attributable to my new solution.

Core Value

Information derived from interview, personas, and comparative analysis allowed met to defined three core values of grocery shopping as follows:

Price

Because most of the potential users are international students, a service with reasonable price would be a primary concern.

Efficiency

Users seek a solution that can save their time in transportation.

Experience

Users still enjoy the experiences of grocery shopping.

Step 3. Ideation

Based on what I have found in previous steps, I sketched 10 different solutions. Later, I presented these sketches to my potential users to solicit their feedback.

Insights from Potential Users

  • The problem with transportation is more prominent than the problem of groceries weight.
  • People enjoy the process of grocery shopping instead of ordering things online.

Final Decision of the Solution

Among the 10 solutions I proposed, the idea of sharing a car was favored by most potential users

Step 4. Defining Design Dimensions

Functional Specification

After deciding the final solution, the next step was defining the detailed functions of the solution. I used a paradgim called Question-Option-Criteria to specify the details of each key function.

  • Users have to log in before using the system.
  • Information will be presented with a list and a map.
  • Only landmarks and intersections are allowed to be the pick-up locations.
  • The system will provide different interfaces for the drivers and the passengers.
  • The system will automatically match up passengers with drivers. However, drivers have the flexibility to make an adjustment.

Step 5. Prototype

Paper Prototype

Paper prototype is a good way to retrieve users' feedbacks before investing a considerable amount of time in building the digital prototyping.I built a paper prototype to test the interaction flow of finding a car for shopping and providing a car.

Discovery

Sources of confusion were found in testing:

  • Users were confused about the differences between functions for a driver and for a passenger.
  • Some of the buttons cannot clearly link to their functions.

Reflection

The major problem of a paper prototype testing was that users tended to experience less frustration as long as every step they took led to a response. This makes it harder for user to discover the problems of interaction.


Mid-fi Prototype/Interaction Map

Building an interaction map helped me visaulize the steps of interaction. I also built a digital mid-fi prototype and tested it with my users.

Digital Prototype and Reflection

In my final digital prototype, I made the interfaces for drivers and passengers more distinct from each other. However, some users still reported confusion about the functions for drivers and users. Being too familiar with my design could be a potential blind spot, preventing me from identifying the core problem. I thought the linkage between figure icons and functions should be made clear and intuitive. Feedback from the first-time user directed me to re-examine every signifier in my design. For example, in my early prototype I placed the “map view” at the top right corner of the screen for user to switch from a list view to a map view. I found some people mistakenly considered the button as an indication of the current mode because of their experiences with other interfaces. I rearranged the location of the buttons accordingly. I also removed the function, which allowed drivers to manually add passengers into their car. As suggested by Users’ feedback, drivers preferred a system which automatically imported passengers on its own.
